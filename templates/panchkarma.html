Panchakarma Therapy

Comprehensive detoxification and rejuvenation program consisting of five therapeutic procedures

What is Panchakarma?

Ancient Ayurvedic detoxification and rejuvenation system

Panchakarma is a comprehensive detoxification and rejuvenation program that eliminates toxins from the body while restoring natural balance. It consists of five main therapeutic procedures, each targeting specific doshas and body systems.

  • Eliminates deep-seated toxins
  • Restores natural balance
  • Rejuvenates body systems
  • Enhances immunity

Treatment Phases:

Purvakarma (Pre-treatment)

  • Consultation and assessment
  • Dietary modifications
3-7 days

Pradhanakarma (Main treatment)

  • Main detox procedure
  • Continuous monitoring
1-5 days

Paschatkarma (Post-treatment)

  • Gradual diet introduction
  • Rejuvenation therapies
7-14 days

The Five Therapies

Vamana Therapy (Therapeutic Emesis)

Cleansing the upper digestive and respiratory tract by controlled emesis

Purpose:

Vamana therapy is a detoxifying process that expels excess Kapha and toxins from the stomach and respiratory tract. It restores balance to the Kapha dosha, enhancing digestion, immunity, and respiratory function.

Procedure Steps:

  • Pre-treatment with Snehana (oleation) and Swedana (fomentation)
  • Administration of herbal emetic formulations (e.g., Madanaphala, Yashtimadhu)
  • Controlled induction of vomiting under supervision
  • Rest, followed by light diet and herbal decoctions for recovery

Benefits:

  • Clears respiratory congestion, asthma, and allergies
  • Improves metabolism and digestive strength
  • Balances Kapha dosha, enhancing energy and vitality
  • Boosts immunity and mental clarity
*Performed under the supervision of a qualified Ayurvedic practitioner.

Raktamokshana Therapy (Bloodletting)

Purification of the blood to remove toxins and balance the Pitta dosha.

Purpose:

Raktamokshana helps eliminate impure blood and reduce excessive Pitta dosha. It detoxifies the circulatory system and promotes skin health.

Procedure Steps:

  • Selection of appropriate bloodletting technique (leech or venesection)
  • Localized cleaning and mild stimulation
  • Controlled removal of impure blood
  • Post-procedure care with antiseptic herbs and light diet

Benefits:

  • Purifies blood and clears skin diseases
  • Reduces inflammation and acne
  • Balances Pitta and improves complexion
  • Enhances overall detoxification
*Should be performed under expert Ayurvedic supervision.

Basti Therapy (Medicated Enema)

Nourishing and detoxifying the colon to balance the Vata dosha.

Purpose:

Basti therapy primarily targets the Vata dosha, helping to cleanse the colon and rejuvenate the body. It restores lubrication in joints, improves digestion, and strengthens the nervous system.

Procedure Steps:

  • Preparation with Snehana (oleation) and Swedana (sudation)
  • Administration of medicated oil or herbal decoction enema
  • Gentle expulsion of toxins from the colon
  • Post-care diet and rejuvenating herbs

Benefits:

  • Relieves constipation and joint stiffness
  • Improves flexibility and nervous system health
  • Balances Vata dosha and enhances vitality
  • Supports detoxification and longevity
*Performed under qualified Ayurvedic supervision only.

Nasya Therapy (Nasal Administration)

Cleansing and nourishing the head region through medicated oil drops in the nose.

Purpose:

Nasya helps eliminate toxins from the sinus, throat, and head region. It improves sensory functions, enhances focus, and brings mental clarity.

Procedure Steps:

  • Face and neck are gently massaged with warm medicated oil.
  • Steam therapy is given to open the nasal channels.
  • Herbal oil drops are administered in each nostril.
  • Patient inhales deeply to ensure full absorption.

Benefits:

  • Relieves headaches, migraines, and sinus congestion
  • Improves breathing and mental clarity
  • Enhances memory and concentration
  • Prevents premature greying and hair fall
*Performed under qualified Ayurvedic supervision only.

Raktamokshana Therapy (Bloodletting)

An Ayurvedic detoxification therapy designed to cleanse the blood and restore balance.

Purpose:

Raktamokshana aims to remove impure blood to alleviate Pitta-related disorders. It purifies the system, supports skin rejuvenation, and promotes overall health.

Procedure Steps:

  • Identify the affected area and perform mild oil massage.
  • Use of sterilized tools or leeches for gentle bloodletting.
  • Extraction of impure blood to cleanse the system.
  • Application of healing herbs and dietary care post-procedure.

Benefits:

  • Improves skin glow and complexion
  • Reduces acne, eczema, and skin rashes
  • Relieves inflammation and joint pain
  • Enhances blood purification and liver function
*Performed only by trained Ayurvedic practitioners in sterile conditions.
